Academics

A CBSE-aligned programme from Nursery to Class XII, taught to understanding rather than to memorisation.

Overview

Four wings, one standard

The school runs four academic wings. Each has its own timetable shape and its own way of assessing progress, but all four answer to the same rule: no child moves ahead with a gap left unclosed.

Wing One

Pre-Primary & Primary · Nursery to Class V

The years where reading, number sense and handwriting are either built properly or patched up for the next decade. We build them properly.

Subjects

  • English — reading, phonics, handwriting, comprehension
  • Hindi — reading, वर्णमाला, comprehension
  • Mathematics — number sense before procedure
  • Environmental Studies (EVS)
  • General Knowledge & Computer basics (from Class III)
  • Art, Music and Physical Education — timetabled, not optional

How the day runs

A single class teacher stays with the group for the core subjects, so nobody falls between two teachers. Fifteen minutes of reading aloud happens every day in every primary classroom. Specialist teachers take art, music and games.

Wing Two

Middle School · Class VI to VIII

Subject teaching begins here. The jump catches a lot of children out, so the first term is deliberately paced to let them find their footing.

Subjects

  • English, Hindi, Sanskrit (third language)
  • Mathematics
  • Science — physics, chemistry and biology taught as one integrated course
  • Social Science — history, civics, geography
  • Computer Science
  • Art Education and Physical Education

What changes from primary

  • A different teacher for each subject, and a named class teacher who tracks the whole child
  • Laboratory work begins — science stops being something read about
  • Written examinations alongside continuous assessment
  • Students join a house and take part in inter-house academics and sport

Wing Three

Secondary · Class IX to X

Two years pointed at the Class X board examination, without turning school into a coaching centre.

Subjects

  • English (Language & Literature)
  • Hindi
  • Mathematics
  • Science — physics, chemistry, biology with full practical work
  • Social Science
  • Information Technology (skill subject)

Board preparation

Pre-board examinations are held under real board conditions. Teachers run remedial slots after school for students who need them — at no extra charge, because a child who needs help should not need a chequebook to get it.

Wing Four

Senior Secondary · Class XI to XII

Three streams. The choice matters more than almost any other decision a student makes here, so it is made with a teacher, not alone.

Assessment

Six checkpoints a year, and no surprises at the end of it

Parents should never learn in March that something went wrong in August. Assessment here is continuous and reported as it happens.

Periodic Tests

Short, syllabus-linked tests through each term. Marks go home the week they are marked.

Half-Yearly

Full written examination in September covering the first term's work.

Practical & Project

Laboratory records, projects and oral work carry real weight, not token marks.

Annual Examination

Held in February. Promotion is decided on the whole year, not this one paper.

Homework

Homework should consolidate what was taught, not outsource the teaching to parents. We cap it deliberately.
